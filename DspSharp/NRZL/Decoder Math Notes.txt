NrzlDecoder computes the area of the bit by examining the samples that contribute to the bit.
It then uses the area of the bit to declare its value after examining every sample that 
contributed to the bit.

Since the bit starts and ends between samples (it'll never perfectly start/end on a sample), 
we have to deal with the boundary conditions of calculating what the y-axis value is at the
start and end of the bit by linearly interpolating between the two samples that contain the 
bit edge.

Consider the following time-series graph of samples s1-s5 and interpolated points x1 & x2:

Interpolated points:
* x1: The end of the previous bit and the start of the current bit.
* x2: The end of the current bit and the start of the next bit.
* Note that points x1 and x2 should be near zero if the DPLL is working 
  well and the data is well behaved.

The bit is exactly between x1 and x2. The bit ends roughly between s4 and s5, closer to s5.

`rst`: remaining symbol time. The amount of time, in units of phase (1.0 phase == 1 bit) before hitting
the end of the bit (x2). When rst is negative, we've found the prevSample and currSample values that straddle 
the end of the bit.

The measuredFreq in this example is roughly 16000/48000 == 0.333; "roughly because its updated as per DPLL logic.
It is written as 333 in the chart above for clarity/simplicity.

          prevSample   currSample    rst          Math logic
Step 1:   s2           s3            333 + 283    Just compute the area between s3 & s2 and add that to the integral.
Step 2:   s3           s4            283          Just compute the area between s4 & s3 and add that to the integral.
Step 3:   s4           s5            -50          Bit ended before s5; split the area to the current bit and the next bit.

In step 1:
   Just compute the area contributed between s2 and s3 and add it to our integral of the bit's area.
   area   = [height]           * [width]
          = [(s2 + s3) / 2.0 ] * 1.0
   bitIntegral += (s2 + s3) / 2.0 

In step 2:
   Same.
   bitIntegral += (s3 + s4) / 2.0

In step 3 
   Have to linearly interpolate point x2 and compute the area between s4 and x2.


`fraction` is the ratio indicating how close the end of the bit was to s5.

                (measuredFreq + rst)    (333 + -50)   283
    fraction =  -------------------- =  ----------- = ---- = 0.849 = 84.9%
                    measuredFreq            333       333

To find x2's y-value, we linearly interpolate between s4 and s5 using fraction. 
 --> The larger fraction is, the more that x2 is closer to s5.

      x2 = s4 * (1-fraction) + s5*(fraction)
         = 700 * (1 - 0.849) + -100 * 0.849 
         = 105.7 + -84.9
         = 20.8

Then, what is the area of the polygon between s4 and x2? 
Its the average of s4 and x2, but we have to pro-rate it because it's not full-width like the width between s3 and s4.

       bitIntegral += fraction * (s4 + x2) / 2.0;

At this point we have the complete integral for the bit. Threshold its value and declare the bit.
       bit = bitIntegral > 0;

Then reset stats and start computing the next bit. The start of the the next bit is the area between x2 and s5.
       bitIntegral = 0.0;
       bitIntegral += (1-fraction) * (x2 + s5)/2.0
